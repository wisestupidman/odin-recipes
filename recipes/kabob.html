Kabob Koobideh Recipe

Description

Kabob Koobideh, or minced meat kabob, is a traditional Iranian kabob/barbecue dish. This simple, yet flavourful dish highlights the flavours of the few ingredients. Traditionally, this dish is cooked in a "manghal" which is a rectangular, open flame barbecue. The meat is cooked using wide and long metal skewers (seekh).The key to success involves a natural, 100% wood charcoal fire/grill- no briquettes or lighter fluid. Kabob koobideh (minced meat kabob) is usually accompanied by grilled tomatoes, white rice or bread (usually pita or an Iranian bread called Sangak) , as well as dried sumac, fresh onions, and parsley.

Ingredients

  • Lean ground Beef or lamb (or a 50/50 mix of both)
  • White Onion
  • Salt
  • Baking Soda
  • Tomatoes
  • White Onions
  • Ground Sumac
  • Pita Bread
  • Fresh Parsley
  1. Grate or blend the onion
  2. Thoroughly mix the grated/blended onion
  3. Add salt
  4. Add a pinch of baking soda
  5. Mix thoroughly
  6. Separate the mixture into balls that fill your hand
  7. Put each ball onto a single skewer and spread evenly to be the width of your grill
  8. Take your whole tomatoes and add a generous pinch of salt, then fit as many as you can onto your skewers
  9. Light your 100% wood coals and get them white hot
  10. Once the coals are hot and there is minimal flame, put your tomato skewers on the grill, rotate often, until there is a char on both sides - you will know when they are fully cooked when they can easily slide off the skewer
  11. Once all the tomatoes are cooked, now it is time to cook the meat skewers. The key to this is to flip often until there is a beautiful char on both sides. When the kabob skewers are cooked, you will be able to pinch them between 2 pieces of bread and the kabob will slide off
  12. Now that everything is cooked, you can make a sandwhich/wrap using the bread, the kebob, tomato, parsley, and sumac (fresh onions can be served to bite as you eat or into the sandwhich)
  13. Enjoy!
